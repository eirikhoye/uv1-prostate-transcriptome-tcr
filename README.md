Title:
T cell transcriptional and receptor signatures predict response to telomerase vaccination in prostate cancer

Abstract:

While prostate cancer (PC) is typically immunologically cold, limiting the efficacy of immune checkpoint inhibitors, therapeutic vaccination targeting tumor-associated antigens represents an attractive strategy to promote disease control in low-volume metastatic patients. The UV1 cancer vaccine is based on immunization with tripeptide fragments from human telomerase reverse transcriptase (hTERT). A phase II clinical trial demonstrated robust T cell induction in men with de novo metastatic castration-sensitive prostate cancer (mCSPC). Comparison with long-term survival data from non-metastatic CSPC patients showed that, despite metastatic disease at diagnosis, UV1-treated patients mounting an early vaccine-induced immune response achieved progression-free and overall survival comparable to non-metastatic patients.

We examined immune and molecular determinants of clinical benefit following UV1 vaccination, including tumor transcriptome and T cell receptor (TCR) profiling from circulating and tissue-resident T cells of the 23 enrolled men. Analysis of diagnostic and post-UV1 treatment biopsies revealed that low baseline T cell exhaustion and higher CD8+ T cell abundance were associated with early immune response to the vaccine and longer survival. TCR repertoires of early responders were enriched for specific motifs relative to non-responders.

These findings indicate that baseline intratumoral T cell exhaustion and repertoire composition shape responsiveness to hTERT vaccination and long-term outcome. Overall, our study highlights how baseline immune profiling may serve as a companion biomarker to identify mCSPC patients most likely to benefit from therapeutic vaccination.
